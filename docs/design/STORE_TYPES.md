# Store Types

Each store type in mallcore-sim is a thematic skin over shared core systems, with one or two unique mechanics that give it a distinct identity. This document details all five planned store types.

---

## 1. Sports Memorabilia

**Vibe**: Wood-paneled walls, framed jerseys, glass display cases, sports radio playing in the background. Smells like old cardboard and polished trophies.

### Item Categories
- **Trading cards**: Singles, packs, sealed boxes. Graded cards (PSA-style 1-10 scale) command premiums.
- **Autographs**: Signed balls, photos, equipment. Authenticity certificates affect value.
- **Jerseys and apparel**: Game-worn vs. replica distinction. Team and player popularity fluctuate.
- **Equipment**: Bats, gloves, helmets. Display pieces more than functional items.
- **Sealed product**: Wax boxes, factory sets. Unopened product appreciates over time.

### Unique Mechanics
- **Season cycle**: Player/team popularity shifts based on an abstract "sports season" system. Buy low on slumping players, sell high on breakout stars.
- **Authentication**: Some items arrive with questionable provenance. The player can pay for authentication -- real items gain value, fakes are worthless.

### Customer Types
- Casual fan (buys team gear, not price-sensitive on low-end items)
- Serious collector (hunts graded cards, willing to pay premium for condition)
- Investor (buys sealed product and high-grade cards as investment)
- Kid with allowance (small budget, excited about packs)

## 2. Retro Game Store

**Vibe**: Neon signs, CRT TV playing demos, shelves crammed with cartridges, posters for games that never existed. Faint hum of old hardware.

### Item Categories
- **Cartridges**: Loose, complete-in-box (CIB), new-in-box (NIB). Condition and completeness are everything.
- **Consoles**: Working vs. for-parts. Some need refurbishment before sale.
- **Strategy guides and magazines**: Supplementary items, low margin but collectors want them.
- **Accessories**: Controllers, memory cards, cables. Utilitarian but necessary stock.
- **Imports**: Japanese exclusives and PAL region variants for dedicated collectors.

### Unique Mechanics
- **Testing station**: Customers can test games/consoles before buying. Increases conversion rate but takes up floor space and time.
- **Refurbishment**: Broken consoles and scratched discs can be repaired in the back room for resale at a markup. Takes time and has a failure chance.

### Customer Types
- Nostalgic adult (looking for childhood favorites, emotional purchases)
- Speedrunner/enthusiast (wants specific titles, knowledgeable about value)
- Parent shopping for kid (needs guidance, budget-conscious)
- Reseller (tries to buy underpriced items, savvy)

## 3. Video Rental

**Vibe**: Blue carpet, fluorescent lights, rows of plastic cases with cover art facing out, a "NEW RELEASES" wall, candy at the register. Pure Blockbuster energy.

### Item Categories
- **VHS tapes**: The core stock. New releases, classics, horror section, foreign films.
- **DVDs**: Premium tier, higher rental price, customers prefer them.
- **Snacks and drinks**: Impulse add-ons at the register, small but consistent margin.
- **Merchandise**: Movie posters, standees. Decorative and for sale.

### Unique Mechanics
- **Rental system**: Items are rented, not sold. Revenue comes from rental fees, and inventory returns (usually). This is the core differentiator from other store types.
- **Late fees**: Customers sometimes return late. Late fees are revenue but hurt reputation if enforced too aggressively.
- **Recommendation engine**: The player can set up "Staff Picks" and themed displays. Good recommendations increase rental frequency for featured titles.
- **Damage and loss**: Tapes get worn out over time and occasionally go missing. Replacement cost is a business expense.

### Customer Types
- Friday night family (rents 2-3 titles plus snacks, predictable)
- Movie buff (rents obscure titles, appreciates good curation)
- Binge renter (takes 5+ titles, high volume, occasionally late)
- New release chaser (only wants the latest, will leave if it's checked out)

## 4. PocketCreatures Card Shop

**Vibe**: Bright colors, tournament tables in the back, binders in glass cases, booster box towers, kids trading on the floor. Fictional TCG inspired by early-2000s card game culture.

### Item Categories
- **Booster packs**: Sealed randomized packs. The gambling-adjacent thrill of opening.
- **Singles**: Individual cards sold from binders. Price based on rarity, playability, and condition.
- **Sealed boxes and cases**: Bulk sealed product for collectors and investors.
- **Accessories**: Sleeves, binders, playmats, deck boxes. Steady margin.
- **Starter decks**: Entry-level product for new players.

### Unique Mechanics
- **Pack opening**: The player can open packs to sell as singles (higher expected value but variable) or sell sealed (lower but guaranteed margin). This risk/reward decision is central.
- **Tournament hosting**: Spend money to host events that attract customers and build reputation. Tournament players buy singles and accessories.
- **Meta shifts**: The PocketCreatures metagame shifts over time, changing which cards are in demand. Savvy players stock up before spikes.

### Customer Types
- Competitive player (buys meta singles, accessories, attends tournaments)
- Collector (wants complete sets, foils, first editions regardless of playability)
- Pack cracker (just wants to open packs, buys in volume)
- Parent buying for kid (needs guidance, buys starter decks and packs)
- Trader (wants to trade rather than buy, occasionally good deals)

## 5. Consumer Electronics

**Vibe**: Chrome shelving, white displays, product boxes stacked high, demo units chained to tables, a wall of headphones. Best Buy meets Sharper Image.

### Item Categories
- **Portable music players**: MP3 players, CD players, minidisc. The iPod era.
- **Portable gaming**: Handheld consoles, cartridges, accessories.
- **Audio**: Headphones, earbuds, portable speakers, clock radios.
- **Gadgets**: Digital cameras, USB drives, PDAs, novelty tech.
- **Accessories**: Cases, chargers, cables, screen protectors. High margin, low excitement.

### Unique Mechanics
- **Demo units**: Setting up working demo units on the floor increases sales for that product category but costs inventory (one unit taken off the shelf).
- **Product lifecycle**: Electronics depreciate as newer models release. Buy early at high margin, clear old stock before it becomes worthless. Opposite of the appreciation curve in collectible stores.
- **Warranty upsell**: Offer extended warranties at the register for bonus margin. Customers occasionally come back with warranty claims.

### Customer Types
- Early adopter (wants the newest thing, price-insensitive)
- Bargain hunter (waits for clearance, buys last-gen)
- Gift buyer (seasonal spikes, needs recommendations, mid-budget)
- Tech enthusiast (knowledgeable, compares specs, buys accessories)

---

## Shared Across All Store Types

- Inventory management (backroom + floor)
- Pricing system (player-set, market-relative)
- Customer AI (browse, evaluate, purchase/leave)
- Reputation tracking
- Day/night cycle
- Store layout and display placement
- Daily financial summary

The goal is that switching between store types feels like playing a different genre of music on the same instrument.
